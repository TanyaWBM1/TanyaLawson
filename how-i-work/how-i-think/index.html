The Pattern

Clarity before action — strategy before tools.
How I frame problems
Start wide. Then narrow.

I begin with context: what’s happening, what’s at stake, and who gets impacted. Then I locate the constraint — the real blocker that makes “simple fixes” fail.

Only after the constraint is clear do I design a plan that’s actionable, compliant, and easy for people to follow under pressure.

What I look for
Where risk lives.

• ambiguous ownership
• missing definitions
• compliance constraints
• timeline pressure
• “tribal knowledge” workflows

Core thinking steps
The sequence I rely on — consistently.
  1. Step back. Pause the noise and get the full picture.
  2. Find the real constraint. Identify what’s truly blocking progress (rules, time, structure, risk).
  3. Turn confusion into a path. Translate complexity into clear steps people can execute.
  4. Design inside the rules. Build a compliant strategy that doesn’t depend on heroics.
  5. Execute + document. Follow through with records that hold up later.
