USE CASES

How decisions get made → how systems get built → how outcomes happen.

These use cases are not just tasks or tools. This website is a live example of my work: taking something unclear and turning it into a clean, structured, decision-ready system. Every section exists for a reason — to reduce confusion, build trust, and make it easy for the right people to say “yes.” Instead of using a pre-built template, I designed and built this site from scratch, learning what I needed in real time. I used AI as a support tool to understand structure, edit code, and iterate quickly, while making the decisions myself. With no prior background in HTML or CSS, I taught myself on the fly — working directly in GitHub, testing changes, fixing what broke, and refining the system until it worked the way it should.

Use Case #1
Problem Framing Decision Clarity

How I Think

This use case shows how I approach complex problems before touching tools or technology. I focus on understanding the real issue, the human pressure involved, the risks, and what success actually looks like.

Thinking Pattern

How I move from chaos to clean execution

  1. Step back. Pause the noise. Get the full picture before acting.
  2. Find the real constraint. Identify what’s truly blocking progress (risk, rules, structure, time).
  3. Turn confusion into a simple path. Convert complexity into clear next steps people can actually follow.
  4. Build a compliant system. Design the structure so it works inside the rules — not against them.
  5. Execute + document. Follow through with clean records so results hold up later.
Includes two real examples
  • 1A: Pro Se Handbook — turning legal confusion into clear next steps for people and small businesses.separating fact from opinion
  • 1B: Used Car Dealership Overhaul — solving a licensing blocker with a practical, compliant “office trailer” solution.spotting conflicts of interest
Why it matters
  • This establishes me as strategic — not reactive — in high-stakes environments.
